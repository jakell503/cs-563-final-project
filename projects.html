BLS Database

This project was for CS 586 Databases where I created a database for managing and querying inflation data from the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The database was built using PostgreSQL which is an open source relational database management system.

The tables contained time series data and was focused on the price of goods, particularly food staples such as milk, eggs, ground beef, and eggs.

The database consisted of five tables with relationships between the tables that were enforced using primary keys, foreign keys, and constraints. This gave the database strong data integrity.

CNN Comparison

This project was for CS 541 Artificial Intelligence and compares a custom shallow CNN with a pretrained DenseNet-121 for classifying five thoracic diseases in the CheXpert chest X-ray dataset.

Both models used the U-Zeros policy for uncertain labels and were trained under identical conditions. DenseNet-121 consistently outperformed the custom CNN, achieving a mean AUC of 0.7979 vs. 0.7335, with the largest gains in Cardiomegaly and Pleural Effusion, which involve clearer structural abnormalities.

For texture-based findings like Consolidation and Edema, performance differences were minimal or slightly negative. DenseNet-121 also converged faster but overfit more quickly, requiring early stopping.

Overall, the results show that transfer learning provides meaningful benefits, especially for structural pathologies, while shallow models may be more robust to noisy or subtle labels. Future improvements could include alternative uncertainty strategies, data augmentation, or testing additional pretrained architectures.
